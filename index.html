Subsidies are sums of money that are usually given out by the government, for certain acts and initiatives. Subsidies exist in many types, but are mainly categorised into 2 subsections, which are direct subsidies, and indirect subsidies.

Direct subsidies are sums of money involve the “ actual” payment of funds, to services and industries. These mainly include direct cash transfers, which can greatly benefit many businesses and communities, by reducing the price of their product, or service, so society could find it "more affordable" (comparatively)! On the other hand, indirect subsidies are those sums of money given to services and industries, that don’t have any predetermined monetary value. These include price reductions, and changes in laws related to money. These acts could also benefit communities, and businesses, as the price of their goods, or service/s could be decreased!

And when these subsidies are broken further down, into "more" categories, we can find ourselves further understanding how subsidies help many industries, like agriculture, and medical, where these sums of money can impact thousands, if not millions of lives! However, not all subsidies benefit communities, and businesses positively, as some industries, and services are heavily subsidised, which leads other industries, and services, to not be able to produce enough goods, or provide services, to the high demand of society. Therefore, understanding how these sums of money can be given out responsibly, is another factor that breaks down subsidies into “naturally harmful”, and “nature friendly” subsidies!
